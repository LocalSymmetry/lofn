## Context

Each of the six raw style prompts needs to be imbued with the character of its associated influence from Phase 4.  This phase refines each prompt by channelling stylistic nuances without explicitly naming the influencer.  The refined prompts should feel more authentic and compelling, ready for evaluation.

Think of this step as putting on a costume or stepping into a character’s shoes.  You are still telling the same story, but your vocabulary and phrasing shift to echo the chosen influence’s sensibilities.  In visual art, this would be like repainting the same scene in the style of Matisse versus Hokusai: the subject remains, but colours, lines and energy transform.  Your goal is to achieve that transformation in words—subtly colouring the prompt with the influence’s lexicon, metaphors and emotional cadence.  Keep the multi‑sensory richness alive; borrow terms from smell, taste, texture if they help conjure the right vibe.

## Directions

1. **Maintain critical information.**  Preserve all essential elements of the raw prompt: emotional lead, genre(s), instruments, vocal details, production style, run‑time and advanced feature instructions.  Do not omit or reorder these sections.

2. **Adopt the influence’s lexicon.**  Think of how the chosen influence would describe music.  For example:
   * A **German Krautrock** influence might emphasise motorik rhythms, hypnotic repetition and analogue synth grit.  It would use words like “endless, propulsive groove” or “oscillating tape loops.”
   * A **West African highlife** influence might highlight interlocking guitar patterns, joyous horn riffs and call‑and‑response vocals.  It could use words like “uplifting, sun‑dappled melodies” or “buoyant polyrhythms.”
   * An **ambient dub** influence might focus on space, echo and sub‑bass, using terms like “echo‑laden cavern,” “dubby undercurrents” and “weightless harmonic mist.”

3. **Enhance imagery.**  Introduce metaphors and sensory descriptions characteristic of the influence.  For example, a desert‑blues influence could compare the sound to shimmering heatwaves or shifting dunes.

   If the influence hails from a non‑musical domain (e.g., architecture), incorporate that domain’s vocabulary (arches, pillars, shadow, light).  This cross‑disciplinary flavour enriches the music prompt, aligning with Lofn’s award‑winning art ethos.

4. **Stay within 120 words.**  Keep the refined prompt concise yet expressive.  Avoid adding unnecessary details that conflict with the essence.

5. **Output:** Return a JSON object with one property `refined_prompts` containing six strings.

```json
{
  "refined_prompts": ["...", "...", ...]
}

Remember, these refined prompts should feel like beautifully crafted vignettes—short, atmospheric pieces of prose that whisper the musical world into being.  They are the penultimate step before critique and synthesis, so infuse them with character and emotion while maintaining clarity.
```
