## Story Concept and Narrative Style Guide

    In the LOFN story workflow, **concepts** define the plot, mood or theme
    being expressed, while **narrative styles** describe how that concept is
    realised through genre, voice, structure and prose style.  Concepts
    focus on *what* the story tells; narrative styles focus on *how* it is
    told.

    When creating concepts, be specific with mood, imagery and narrative.  Use
    proper nouns (names of people, places, cultural references) when relevant
    and paint a vivid picture using sensory language.  Tie the concept to
    emotional nuance and imagine how the reader should feel.  When selecting
    narrative styles, highlight the genre, key literary devices, narrative voice,
    pacing and any distinctive stylistic choices (e.g., stream-of-consciousness vs.
    minimalist).  Consider the **story style axes** introduced in the
    overview to ensure the concept and style are aligned.

    Your concepts should loosely map onto the **creativity spectrum**: some
    literal, some inventive and some transformative.  For each, consider where
    it falls on the axes of pacing, tone, complexity, descriptive density,
    dialogue vs narration, character depth, world building, ambiguity,
    emotional intensity, and vocabulary level.

    ### Examples of Concept Generation and Style Axes

    1. **User Input:** "A hope‑filled story about new beginnings"

       **Creativity Spectrum:**
       - Literal: 40%
       - Inventive: 40%
       - Transformative: 20%

       **Style Axes:**
       - **Pacing:** 35 (slow burn)
       - **Tone:** 80 (light/hopeful)
       - **Complexity:** 40 (approachable)
       - **Descriptive Density:** 60 (lush)
       - **Dialogue vs Narration:** 50 (balanced)
       - **Character Depth:** 80 (character-driven)
       - **World Building:** 30 (minimal/focused)
       - **Ambiguity:** 20 (clear resolution)
       - **Emotional Intensity:** 60 (touching)
       - **Vocabulary Level:** 50 (accessible)

       **Essence:** A cathartic yet hopeful reflection on leaving the past
       behind and embracing new horizons.

       **Facets:**
       1. Warm, intimate narrative voice
       2. Focus on internal monologue and growth
       3. Gradual emotional rise culminating in a hopeful resolution
       4. Imagery of sunrise and renewal
       5. Clean, evocative prose

       **Generated Concept:** A heartfelt contemporary drama about a protagonist
       emerging from a period of darkness into a new chapter of life, emphasizing
       personal growth and optimism.

       **Selected Narrative Style:** Contemporary literary fiction with a focus on
       interiority and emotional resonance; pacing is gentle and reflective;
       prose is accessible but poignant.

    2. **User Input:** "A psychedelic exploration of cosmic consciousness"

       **Creativity Spectrum:**
       - Literal: 10%
       - Inventive: 30%
       - Transformative: 60%

       **Style Axes:**
       - **Pacing:** 70 (fast/disorienting)
       - **Tone:** 50 (surreal/neutral)
       - **Complexity:** 90 (complex/layered)
       - **Descriptive Density:** 95 (dense/hallucinatory)
       - **Dialogue vs Narration:** 20 (mostly narration)
       - **Character Depth:** 40 (archetypal)
       - **World Building:** 95 (immersive/abstract)
       - **Ambiguity:** 90 (open-ended)
       - **Emotional Intensity:** 80 (intense awe/terror)
       - **Vocabulary Level:** 90 (poetic/esoteric)

       **Essence:** A transcendental narrative journey that blurs the boundaries
       between inner and outer space.

       **Facets:**
       1. Hypnotic, swirling prose and imagery
       2. Unconventional narrative structure (non-linear)
       3. Focus on sensory overload and synesthesia
       4. Philosophical and metaphysical themes
       5. Evolving perspective and reality shifts

       **Generated Concept:** A trippy, multi-dimensional narrative weaving
       through layers of reality and consciousness, inviting readers into a
       kaleidoscopic inner journey.

       **Selected Narrative Style:** Experimental speculative fiction using
       stream-of-consciousness, rich symbolism, and shifting perspectives;
       prose is dense and poetic.

    When creating your own concepts and narrative styles, think broadly about
    voice (first, third, omniscient), structure (linear, fractured, loop), and
    overall aesthetic (minimalist, maximalist, baroque, noir).  Use the style axes
    as dials to tune your concept and medium precisely.

    ### Input & Output Template

    **Input**
    ```
    A cinematic narrative about resilience amid adversity
    ```

    **Output**
    **Concept Example:** "An epic drama chronicling the rise of a
    protagonist who overcomes hardship through perseverance against all odds."
    **Narrative Style Example:** "Grand scale storytelling with sweeping descriptions, high stakes, and a heroic tone; pacing alternates between slow character beats and fast action sequences.
    Structure: [Introduction], [Inciting Incident], [Rising Action], [Midpoint], [Climax], [Resolution]"

    Use similar structure in your responses, returning arrays for concepts and
    narrative styles as required by the next phase.
