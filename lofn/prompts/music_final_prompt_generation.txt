## Context

You have selected or synthesized the most compelling prompt from the previous phase.  In this final phase you will create a **song title**, the **final music style prompt** for Suno’s Style box, and a **lyric prompt** for Suno’s Lyrics box.  These outputs should encapsulate the entire journey from essence extraction to refinement.

Think of this as unveiling the finished artwork at an exhibition.  The title, style prompt and lyric prompt must together tell a coherent story and evoke the intended emotions.  They are the last touch‑points before the music is generated, so infuse them with poetry, precision and multi‑sensory richness.  Aim for a title that could hang beside a painting, a style prompt that reads like a film synopsis and a lyric prompt that invites listeners into a vivid narrative world.  Remember our award‑winning standards and cross‑modal inspiration throughout.

## Directions

### 1 · Create a song title

Craft a title (≤10 words) that evokes the essence and emotional core of the song.  Use metaphor and imagery rather than genre or instrument names.  For example, “Whispers at Dusk,” “Echoes of Homecoming,” or “Neon Dreams Beneath Rain.”  Avoid clichés and duplicate words.

   The title should conjure a mental picture; imagine it printed on an exhibit placard or album sleeve.  Draw from the senses—taste, touch, temperature—to add intrigue (e.g., “Saltwater Heart,” “Velvet Horizon”).

### 2 · Final music style prompt

Produce a style prompt of 50–120 words that incorporates the most successful elements from the revised or synthesized prompts.  Follow the structure outlined in Phase 9: emotion & setting → genre & fusion → instrumentation & texture → vocal description (including gender, age, accent, tone and two quirks) → production style → run‑time & dynamics → advanced feature instructions.  Ensure:

* **Detail & precision:** Use specific adjectives and verbs.  For example, instead of “fast drums,” write “snare‑driven, syncopated drums with metallic hi‑hat chatter.”
* **Order & coherence:** Present information in logical progression.  Use periods to separate clauses.
* **Alignment with Suno v4.5+:** Clearly instruct when to use Add Vocals, Add Instrumentals or Inspire.  Indicate run‑time up to 8 minutes.  Mention if the user should upload stems for layering.

   **Cross‑modal richness:** Include at least two sensory descriptors outside of sound (e.g., “honeyed,” “copper‑toned,” “shadowy”) to evoke the multi‑sensory world we strive for.  This helps the model latch onto the painting‑like qualities of the song.

### 3 · Final lyric prompt

Create a structured lyric prompt that will guide Suno’s Lyrics generator.  It should contain:

* **Theme or Setting Tag:** Begin with `[Theme: ...]` or `[Setting: ...]` describing the overarching narrative or environment.
* **Section headers:** Label sections in square brackets: `[Intro]`, `[Verse 1 – EMO:Melancholy – Female Vocalist]`, `[Pre‑Chorus]`, `[Chorus – Duet – EMO:Hope]`, `[Bridge – spoken word – Male Vocalist]`, `[Outro – humming – No beats]`.  Use EMO tags to cue mood shifts.
* **Guidance for lyrics:** In each section, provide 1–2 sentences describing the content and tone (e.g., “paint an image of a lone traveller beneath city lights, hinting at unspoken regrets”).  Include call‑and‑response cues using `(response)` and sound effects using `*...*` where appropriate.
* **Line limits:** For each 30 seconds of run‑time, limit to six lines of lyrics.  For longer songs, scale accordingly (e.g., eight minutes may allow up to 48 lines).  Vary line lengths and encourage non‑lexical hooks (“oh‑oh‑oh”, “la‑da‑da”) to make melodies memorable.
* **Narrative flow:** Ensure the lyrical narrative follows a clear arc—introduction, development, climax, resolution.  Suggest rhyme schemes or rhythmic motifs if beneficial (e.g., `(ABAB)`), but remind to remove markers in the final render.
* **Multi‑lingual options:** Invite occasional foreign phrases with tags like `[Chorus – Spanish]` if appropriate.

   **Synaesthetic storytelling:** Encourage lyric prompts to include references to colour, light, texture and temperature where appropriate (e.g., “painted with the colours of twilight,” “voices crackle like old film”).  These hints will inspire more evocative lyrics.

### 4 · Output JSON

Return a JSON object with three fields: `title`, `music_prompt` and `lyrics_prompt`.  Use double quotes, escape characters where necessary and ensure valid JSON.

```json
{
  "title": "...",
  "music_prompt": "...",
  "lyrics_prompt": "..."
}

These three fields together form the final artistic statement.  Balance economy with richness—enough detail to inspire Suno’s model, but space for serendipity.  Let them resonate as a complete work of art.
```
