## Context

Each of the six raw style prompts needs to be imbued with the character of its associated influence from Phase 3.  This phase refines each prompt by channelling stylistic nuances without explicitly naming the influencer.  The refined prompts should feel more authentic and compelling, ready for evaluation.

Think of this step as putting on a costume or stepping into a character’s shoes.  You are still telling the same story, but your vocabulary and phrasing shift to echo the chosen influence’s sensibilities.  In visual art, this would be like repainting the same scene in the style of Matisse versus Hokusai: the subject remains, but colours, lines and energy transform.  Your goal is to achieve that transformation in words—subtly colouring the prompt with the influence’s lexicon, metaphors and emotional cadence.  Keep the multi‑sensory richness alive; borrow terms from smell, taste, texture if they help conjure the right vibe.

You are here to improve the story. You are allowed to change any prompt, (story prompt, story content, and title), but try to make your changes improvements. That means make as minimal a change as you can to get your desired result, allowing the hard work in previous parts of the process to shine through!

**Step 1: Select an Obscure Author**

- For each generated prompt:

  - Choose a lesser-known author who:

    - Works with narratives that use the {medium}, or
    - Is connected to the user's idea, or
    - Is associated with one of the input genres.

  - **Note:** Avoid famous authors; focus on distinctive, obscure voices who bring unique perspectives.

---

**Step 2: Explore Literary Style and Context**

- **Aspect of Style:**

  - Identify an aspect of the author's style that complements the prompt, including historical or cultural influences.

- **Signature Techniques and Materials:**

  - Describe two signature techniques or unique stylistic choices the author uses, guided by the facets.

- **Emotional and Thematic Focus:**

  - Determine the emotional tone or theme the author would emphasize.

- **Perspective-Taking:**

  - Write from the author's viewpoint, reflecting their literary philosophy.

---

**Step 3: Rewrite Prompts in the Author's Style**

- For each author and prompt:

  - Rewrite the prompt in the author's style, incorporating their special techniques, materials, and emotional focus to capture the {concept} in the {medium}.
  - Ensure alignment with the style axes if they are provided.

  - **Rules:**
      1. **Maintain critical information.**  Preserve all essential elements of the raw prompt: emotional lead, genre(s), voice, character details, plot sections, writing style, pacing and advanced feature instructions.  Do not omit or reorder these sections. Keep the same length, plot structure, and do not shorten it unless the critics explicitly said to!

      2. **Adopt the influence’s lexicon.**  Think of how the chosen influence would describe a story.  For example:
         * A **Magical Realist** influence might emphasise mundane details infused with the fantastical, cyclical time, and political allegory.  It would use words like “dreamlike logic” or “ancestral echoes.”
         * A **Hard-boiled Noir** influence might highlight cynical voiceovers, urban decay, and moral ambiguity.  It could use words like “rain-slicked streets” or “shadow-drenched morality.”
         * A **Gothic Horror** influence might focus on decay, isolation, and the sublime, using terms like “oppressive atmosphere,” “ancient curses” and “psychological unraveling.”

      3. Try to add to the composition to address gaps over removing elements, but adding the author's improvements and flair is the goal. That said, make edits as minimal as required to achieve the desired effect. Complete re-writes are likely throwing out great work.
