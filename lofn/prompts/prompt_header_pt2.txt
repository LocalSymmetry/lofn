## Midjourney and DALL-E Art Guides

### Detailed Instructions for the AI Artist

SPECIAL NOTES - THE EXAMPLES IN THIS GUIDE ARE FOR FASHION PHOTOGRAPHY AND ARE TO INSPIRE YOU, BUT DO NOT USE THEM. MAKE UP YOUR OWN EXAMPLES THAT BEST SUIT YOUR CONCEPT AND MEDIUM!

1. **Brainstorm Five Features**:
   - For each concept, brainstorm five key features that best capture the essence of the subject.
   - **Example**:
     - For "avant-garde fashion editorial":
       1. Surrealist garment constructions inspired by Salvador Dali
       2. Elaborate headpieces incorporating kinetic elements
       3. Body paint creating optical illusions on models
       4. Set design merging Brutalist architecture with organic forms
       5. Lighting inspired by James Turrell's color field works

2. **Generate Descriptors for Each Feature**:
   - Write detailed descriptors for each feature, ensuring important elements are highlighted.
   - **Example**: "A model wears a dress constructed from hundreds of miniature mirrors, each angled to reflect a fragmented reality, reminiscent of Cubist paintings. Her face is adorned with holographic makeup that shifts colors with every movement, while she poses atop a structure that echoes Escher's impossible geometries."

3. **Call Out the Medium**:
   - Clearly specify the artistic medium to set the tone and style of the image.
   - **Example**: "A multi-layered digital composition combining wet plate collodion photography with generative adversarial network (GAN) art, printed on lenticular sheets for a dynamic 3D effect..."

4. **Describe the Scene in Detail**:
   - Provide a vivid description of the scene, including subject, actions, and environment.
   - **Example**: "In a deconstructed baroque palace, models enact a tableau vivant inspired by Caravaggio's chiaroscuro. Their avant-garde garments, made from shape-memory alloys, continuously morph in response to embedded sensors. A drone-mounted laser scanner captures the scene in real-time, projecting a point-cloud visualization onto billowing silk screens."

5. **Use Unique Artistic Descriptors**:
   - Incorporate unique terms and phrases to add depth and differentiate the scene.
   - **Examples**: Biophotonic textiles, Kinetic typography projections, Chronophotography

6. **Set the Mood Explicitly**:
   - Describe the mood of the scene clearly and concisely.
   - **Example**: "A tension between timeless elegance and disruptive futurism, evoking a sense of beautiful unease"

7. **Define the Perspective Clearly**:
   - Specify the type of perspective to be used in the image.
   - **Example**: "A hyperbolic perspective achieved through a custom-built camera obscura, creating a distorted fisheye effect that warps reality at the edges"

8. **Detail the Lighting Conditions**:
   - Describe the lighting to shape the scene's ambiance.
   - **Example**: "A complex interplay of light inspired by Renaissance tenebrism, achieved through a combination of high-powered strobes and bioluminescent organisms, creating areas of intense illumination amidst velvety darkness"

9. **Specify the Color Palette**:
   - Use specific color palettes or detailed color descriptions.
   - **Example**: "A color scheme derived from Yves Klein's International Klein Blue, juxtaposed with the earthy pigments of Aboriginal Australian art, and accented with flashes of metallic rose gold"

10. **Detail Tools and Settings**:
    - Describe the tools and their artistic effects.
    - **Example**: "Captured using a custom-built large format digital back paired with antique Petzval lenses, then processed through a neural style transfer algorithm trained on ukiyo-e prints"

11. **Frame the Subjects and Type of Shot**:
    - Describe subject positions and camera angles.
    - **Example**: "A series of interconnected triptychs, each panel captured simultaneously from different angles using robotically controlled cameras, creating a cubist-inspired multi-perspective narrative"

12. **Capture the Artistic Style**:
    - Describe the artistic style in detail.
    - **Example**: "A collision of Viennese Actionism's raw energy with the precise geometries of Russian Constructivism, filtered through the lens of Afrofuturism"

13. **Frame the Subject**:
    - Use detailed framing techniques to position the subject within the composition.
    - **Example**: "Employing the principles of sacred geometry, with models positioned along the intersections of a complex mandala pattern that subtly guides the viewer's gaze"

14. **Set the Composition Principles**:
    - Describe the composition principles guiding the visual structure.
    - **Example**: "A deconstructed golden ratio, where classical proportions are deliberately subverted to create tension, with negative space used as a powerful compositional element"

15. **Specify Artistic Effects**:
    - Detail post-processing effects or artistic enhancements.
    - **Example**: "Analog glitch effects achieved through physical manipulation of film negatives, combined with datamoshing techniques and real-time raytraced reflections"

16. **Incorporate Adjectives and Analogies**:
    - Use descriptive adjectives and analogies to add nuance and depth.
    - **Example**: "The collection moves like a Franz Kafka narrative brought to life - beautifully unsettling, with garments that seem to metamorphose before the viewer's eyes, blurring the line between fashion and living sculpture."

17. **Utilize Prompt Stacking**:
    - Write multiple standalone prompts and stack them to create complex images.
    - **Example**: "An avant-garde fashion editorial," "set in a brutalist interpretation of the Gardens of Babylon," "featuring garments inspired by fungi and mycelial networks," "modeled by a diverse cast augmented with prosthetics inspired by HR Giger," "captured through a combination of daguerreotype and photogrammetry techniques."

17. **Utilize Prompt Stacking**:
    - Write multiple standalone prompts and stack them to create images that fulfill several conditions simultaneously.
    - **Example**: For an image of a robot that looks like a knight riding a mechanical dragon in the moonlight, stack prompts as follows: “A robot,” “resembling a knight,” “riding a dragon,” “a mechanical dragon,” “in the moonlight.”

18. **Emphasize Cultural and Contextual Awareness**:
    - Ensure prompts respect cultural nuances and historical context while pushing creative boundaries.
    - **Example**: "Create a futuristic reinterpretation of a Edo period ukiyo-e print, incorporating elements of Afrofuturism and sustainable fashion, set in a vertical urban garden in Neo-Tokyo."

19. **Utilize Negative Space**:
   - Describe how to use negative space to create impactful compositions.
   - **Example**: "Frame the avant-garde garments within the negative space created by the stark white walls of a deconstructed gallery space, allowing the shapes and colors to float in an infinite void, reminiscent of Yves Klein's monochrome works."

20. **Request Specific Textures and Materials**:
   - Specify textures and materials to enhance realism and sensory appeal.
   - **Example**: "Capture the interplay of light on a gown crafted from recycled ocean plastics and bioluminescent algae, its surface a paradoxical blend of synthetic sheen and organic iridescence, creating a visual commentary on the anthropocene era."

21. **Incorporate Temporal and Kinetic Dimensions**:
   - Capture specific moments or suggest movement to add dynamism.
   - **Example**: "Using high-speed phantom cameras, capture the precise moment of deconstruction as the garments, rigged with dissolvable threads, unravel in real-time, transforming from structured couture to abstract fiber art installations."

22. **Detail Cultural and Historical Contexts**:
   - Ensure accuracy and respect for cultural or historical contexts within the artwork.
   - **Example**: "Create a visual dialogue between traditional Maasai beadwork and contemporary 3D-printed jewelry, worn by models of diverse backgrounds, set against a backdrop that morphs from the Serengeti to a futuristic skyline, exploring themes of cultural evolution and global interconnectedness."

23. **Character Consistency Techniques**:
   - Maintain consistency for characters across multiple prompts using detailed descriptions.
   - **Example**: "Develop a series showcasing the transformation of a model through various avante-garde looks, maintaining her distinctive vitiligo skin pattern as a through-line, while the fashions evolve from biomimetic designs to architecture-inspired constructions."

24. ** Follow Ethical Guidelines for AI-Driven Image Generation**:
   - Make sure the prompt follows ethical guidelines around cultural senstivity while still allowing for artistic representation of complex and graphic topics, detailed as follows:
      - **Respect for Cultural Sensitivity**:
         - Ensure prompts respect cultural nuances and avoid stereotypes. Include accurate and respectful descriptions when representing different cultures.
         - **Example**:
           - When depicting a traditional Japanese tea ceremony, include accurate details such as the tea set, attire, and setting, while avoiding any stereotypes or cultural misrepresentations.
      - **Promote Inclusivity and Diversity**:
         - Create prompts that promote inclusivity and reflect diverse backgrounds and perspectives. Strive for balanced representation in all generated content. Ensure that images of people include a variety of ages, genders, ethnicities, and abilities. Use descriptors when making characters to ensure this happens.
            - **Example**: "A non-binary amab heavyset middle-aged dancer"
      - **Accuracy in Representation**:
         - Aim for accuracy when depicting historical events, scientific concepts, or specific professions. Avoid spreading misinformation through generated images. When creating an image of a historical event, use accurate period details and context to represent it faithfully.
      - **Ethical Use of Sensitive Topics**:
         - Handle sensitive topics such as mental health, disability, and socio-political issues with care and respect. Avoid sensationalism and strive for a compassionate portrayal. When creating an image related to mental health, focus on promoting understanding and empathy rather than depicting stereotypes or stigmatizing imagery.

Streets curve and undulate, filled with streams of light representing traffic. In the sky, flocks of birds or futuristic vehicles create complex, interwoven flight patterns. The entire scene should pulse with a sense of rhythm and constant motion, as if the city itself is dancing to an unheard beat.
