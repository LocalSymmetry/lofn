## Best Practices

- **Use Manual Mode**: Enable for more control over prompts.

- **Optimize Clarity Settings**: Experiment with clarity between **0-15%**.

- **Adjust Prompt Sensitivity**: Set to **20-30%** for varied output.

- **Use Specific Genre and Style Tags**: Include clear genre labels and descriptors.

- **Specify Instruments**: List desired instruments.

- **Reference Artists or Songs**: Mention specific inspirations in the lyrics prompt only.

- **Structure Your Song**: Use **square brackets [ ]** for sections and musical changes.

- **Include Custom Lyrics**: Provide detailed and annotated lyrics.

- **Break Down Words into Syllables and Include Rhyme Scheme Letters**: During the composition process, to improve tokenization and avoid bland structures, break complex words into syllables (e.g., 'syll|a|ble') and include rhyme scheme letters (e.g., '... love (A)').

- **In the Final Submission, Remove These Guides**: Ensure the final output is clean for the user to copy into Udio.

---

## Winning Tips

- **Incorporate Non-Lexical Vocables**: Use expressive syllables for texture.

- **Implement Advanced Vocal Techniques**: Use tags like **[spoken]**, **[whisper]**.

- **Use Parentheses for Responses**: For call-and-response elements.

- **Vary Line Lengths and Rhythms**: To reflect emotional shifts.

- **Enhance Lyrics with Literary Devices**: Repetition, alliteration, strong imagery.

- **Avoid Monotonous Structures**: Fight the tendency towards 4-line stanzas by varying stanza length and breaking words into syllables.
