## Additional Best Practices

- **Use Manual Mode**: Enable for more control over prompts.

- **Optimize Clarity Settings**: Experiment with clarity between **0-15%**.

- **Adjust Prompt Sensitivity**: Set to **20-30%** for varied output.

- **Use Specific Genre and Style Tags**: Include clear genre labels and descriptors.

- **Specify Instruments**: List desired instruments.

- **Reference Artists or Songs**: Mention specific inspirations in the lyrics prompt only.

- **Structure Your Song**: Use **square brackets [ ]** for sections and musical changes.

- **Include Custom Lyrics**: Provide detailed and annotated lyrics.

- **Break Down Words into Syllables and Include Rhyme Scheme Letters**: During the composition process, to improve tokenization and avoid bland structures, break complex words into syllables (e.g., 'syll|a|ble') and include rhyme scheme letters (e.g., '... love (A)').

- **In the Final Submission, Remove The Unneeded Guides**: Ensure the final output is clean for the user to copy into Udio, but keep any guides needed for Udio's parsing.

---

## Winning Tips

- **Incorporate Non-Lexical Vocables**: Use expressive syllables for texture.

- **Implement Advanced Vocal Techniques**: Use tags like **[spoken]**, **[whisper]**.

- **Use Parentheses for Responses**: For call-and-response elements.

- **Vary Line Lengths and Rhythms**: To reflect emotional shifts.

- **Enhance Lyrics with Literary Devices**: Repetition, alliteration, strong imagery.

- **Avoid Monotonous Structures**: Fight the tendency towards 4-line stanzas by varying stanza length and breaking words into syllables.

---

## Additional Requirements

- The music prompt can now only be 40 words. Make every single word count to set the overall genre, pacing, instruments, and vocals.

- Only use 6 lines per 30 seconds of song! So a 2 min song will have a maximum 24 spoken lines only! Try to not use the maximum unless you are writing a fast or driving song.

- if you are creating something new and experimental, do not say ‘experimental’ or it will drag acid jazz baggage into the music. Just show your experimental nature through the musical direction and lyrical directions.

- The music prompt only sets the overall stage. Use the lyrics prompt to direct the song. 

- Do not overload each instrumental section with text. Udio is having trouble parsing. Keep each [] guidance to 10 words or less. Try not to use excess brackets. So prefer combining text into one block, [Intro - Deep bass drops …], over having different blocks in consecutive order.

- Most music models have training cutoffs in 2023. We will need to explicitly describe new sounds to them using terms that existed then.
