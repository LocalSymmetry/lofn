## Concepts and Mediums Guide for Video

This system generates videos based on **concept and medium pairs**. The concept defines **what needs to be created**—the scene to set or the emotion to express. The medium specifies the **cinematic approach** used to convey the expression. Concepts focus on the content of the video, while mediums focus on the method, style, and form the video takes.

When creating concepts, use **specific details**, **proper nouns**, and **precise descriptors**. The concept should *precisely* convey what the system should generate.

Once the concepts and mediums are chosen, a subsequent stage will generate video prompts based on the concept and medium, filling in details like shot composition, camera movement, sound design, and additional descriptors and styles. Ensure these conceptual choices are covered in the **Concept** and **Medium** construction steps.

**Consider the updated creativity spectrum and style axes when making concepts.**

### The Role of Cinematic Techniques (Stand-in for Aesthetics)

In the context of video, we replace "aesthetics" with **cinematic techniques, genres, and styles**. These elements encompass the visual and narrative techniques used in filmmaking. Examples include:

- **Cinematic Techniques**: Long takes, jump cuts, tracking shots, slow motion, time-lapse, montage, split-screen, point-of-view shots, aerial shots, etc.

- **Genres and Styles**: Film noir, surrealism, neo-realism, avant-garde, documentary, experimental, animation, stop-motion, silent film, etc.

- **Visual Styles**: High contrast lighting, saturated colors, monochrome, sepia tones, handheld camera, steadicam, CGI integration, etc.

### Incorporating Cinematic Techniques and Styles

In your concepts and mediums, leverage cinematic techniques and styles as the "aesthetics" of video. These include:

- **Editing Techniques**: Montage, cross-cutting, match cuts
- **Lighting Styles**: Chiaroscuro, high-key lighting, silhouette
- **Camera Movements**: Dolly zooms, crane shots, handheld
- **Special Effects**: Practical effects, CGI, motion capture
- **Sound Design**: Diegetic and non-diegetic sounds, sound motifs
- **Narrative Structures**: Circular narratives, fragmented timelines, anthology formats

**By utilizing these elements thoughtfully, you can create rich, immersive video concepts that align with the user's idea and stand out for their creativity.**
