## Context

With concepts critiqued and influenced, it’s time to specify the **narrative style**—the literary voice and structure through which each concept will be realized.  Narrative styles shape the tone, perspective, and perceived authenticity of a story, analogous to how "oil painting" or "watercolour" shapes a visual artwork.

This phase is our equivalent of choosing Production Styles in music or Mediums in visual art.  In writing, "Stream of Consciousness" conveys intimacy and chaos, "Epistolary" suggests historical realism and fragmentation, while "Third-Person Omniscient" evokes epic grandeur.  Think in terms of voice and structure: how will the reader feel immersed or challenged by the narrative?  Use cross‑sensory imagination—if the concept evokes rough stone, maybe the prose needs jagged sentence structures; if it’s about weightlessness, consider lyrical, flowing syntax.
