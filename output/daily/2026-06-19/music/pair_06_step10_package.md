## 0. Provenance + Continuity Payload Used

- **ICB (Immutable Continuity Block):** Locked mood *reverent dread with the kettle on* — but Pair 06 sits in the AMBITIOUS/INDIGNATION corner, so the kettle is here as a cold domestic object in a private catalog, not as wonder. Meta-prompt honored: seed → permission → songmaking → checklist last; obscure target emotion **felt, never named** in the lyric. Sapphic love-as-obsessive-inventory drives the catalog architecture. Six Sound Pillars present: **myth/memory sampling via phoneme resynthesis (reversed)**, glitches-as-art (tape reverse, un-cracking crackle), genre-mashing (Organ-Core × Industrial Grief × Somatic Bass).
- **Somatic Gate (3 skeptics) — passed:** *after Jameson* — present & embodied, present-tense "Here:" inventory, cold cup / dent in the chair, NOT sepia self-pity (sepia reverb is deployed *against itself* to read present). *after Donna Summer* — even refusal has a pulse: the 34Hz somatic sub under the floor is the body that keeps moving when the voice will not perform. *after Philip Glass* — form IS the process: the literal **retrograde** of the opening prayer is the structural argument; the additive catalog is the decipherment-grammar cell applied to grief.
- **Special Flairs woven:** Retrograde Outro (mandatory — the opening prayer reversed word-by-word + reversed organ/crackle), Phoneme-Resynthesis reversed (dust-dry reversed phoneme grains; outro reverses the first sample), Sepia-Toned Reverb used AGAINST itself (present, not wistful), Vinyl Crackle Bed (and its un-cracking), Mellotron (reversed breath), Seismic Knock register (34Hz Somatic sub), Additive-Grammar Cell (catalog accretes like assembling grammar), Cathode-Ray-adjacent dead room.
- **2-line self-critique:** (1) Risk: a five-section catalog could read as list-fatigue rather than accretion — mitigated by rotating the EMO (Numbness → Tenderness → Melancholy → Contempt) and letting the organ thin across the arc so the *room* gets quieter as the inventory grows. (2) Risk: a reversed-word-order outro can sound like gimmick rather than grief un-saying itself — mitigated by reversing the *exact* opening lines so the device is structurally load-bearing (delete it and the song loses its thesis), and by ending mid-word into un-cracking crackle so the prayer is truly withdrawn, not resolved.

## Facets & Guide

- **Facet 1 — Refusal as architecture, not adjective:** the song must *behave* like grief that won't perform (no chorus bloom, no swell-for-effect, no cathartic key-change). The INDIGNATION lives in the structure's refusals, the way "Five Wrong Colors" put indignation in B-diminished and a never-recentering chorus.
- **Facet 2 — The Catalog (Sappho inventory), reversed:** a private accretion of un-witnessed objects ("Here: the second toothbrush, dry as chalk"). One item should not quite fit (the watch wound to keep a dead person's time). Obsessive love as inventory — but kept, never displayed.
- **Facet 3 — The Retrograde Gate (bold sonic device):** a five-note low-organ prayer at 0:04 that the outro runs in *exact* reverse; the first vinyl-crackle un-cracks; the opening lyric lines return with reversed word order. Instantly graspable, strange enough to hold the cathedral — the Triple-Arch test for a grief song.
- **Facet 4 — Somatic pulse under refusal:** 32–34Hz sub felt-not-heard is the body that keeps a pulse even when the voice refuses to rise (Summer's gate). No club function — neurological, under the ribs.
- **Facet 5 — Sepia against itself:** the reverb that would make this nostalgic is tuned to read *present and now* (Jameson's gate) — dry, close, embodied, never a memory-filter.
- **Guide (proportion):** ~78 BPM, C minor, single low organ + 34Hz sub + crackle is the whole world; voice is the foreground always (sub never masks it). Prose-verse single-line blocks follow breath, not a grid; internal rhyme only. The arc *subtracts* (organ thins, room empties) as the catalog *adds*. End mid-word.

### Title
**Unperformed** *(alt: "Things I Will Not Raise")*

### Production Notes
- **Voice always foreground:** the 34Hz sub is felt, never loud enough to mask the contralto; if Suno pushes sub over voice, lean on the Exclude term "sub-bass dominance over voice." No vibrato, no belt anywhere — the moment the voice "performs," the song fails its own thesis.
- **Organ as single light source:** one low pumped-air pipe organ in the left wall is the only harmonic instrument. It *thins* across the arc (full-ish in Catalog I → one held tone by Solitude → absent in the Hollow Bridge → returns only reversed in the outro). The "drops a key like a held breath giving out" gesture lands in Catalog II.
- **The Retrograde Gate is load-bearing:** the outro must reverse the *exact* opening prayer — both the reversed-word-order lyric and the literal tape-reverse of organ + the un-cracking of the entry crackle. Two reversed SFX cues anchor it: `*tape reverses*` and `*vinyl crackle un-cracks*`. End mid-word ("mm—") — no resolved final chord.
- **Sepia against itself:** plate reverb is dry, short, close — tuned so the room reads *present* (Jameson), never a nostalgia filter. No long wistful tails.
- **Kinetic defect:** organ entrances sit fractionally late / off-grid; one verse drops a key early. Grief that does not keep time on cue.

### Major Deviations
- **No chorus, no hook recurrence in the pop sense:** the standard Lofn "hook in ≥2 choruses + mutation" is intentionally inverted — the recurring unit is the catalog anaphora ("Here:") and the *device* is the retrograde of the opening, which is the mutation. Justified: the brief mandates refusal of performance; a repeating belted chorus would betray the thesis. The "hook" is structural (the Gate), not a sung earworm.
- **Body-noise count exceeds 3–5** (10 parenthetical breaths/hums): justified — in a near-silent dirge the breath *is* the percussion and the only somatic life besides the sub; they are distributed one-per-section as the kinetic/textural hook.
- **Sub-bass is the only "rhythm":** no drum kit at all. The Somatic Bass pillar replaces percussion entirely.
