Second risk: naming the emotion; I kept "the ache of making what you cannot hold" entirely off the page — it has to be inferred from the empty slot and the verbs *kept / set down / cannot carry*.

## Facets & Guide

- **Sonic thesis:** an archivist's inventory that you can *hear her hands in* — each made thing set onto a shelf of light, dry and close, then bloomed wide; the song is the act of keeping, and keeping is the only touch she gets.
- **Bold sonic device (Triple-Arch equivalent):** **the Keeping-Shelf** — a Mellotron-flute chord that bloom-opens each stanza as a new item is set down left-to-right; instantly graspable (a shelf of sound) yet strange enough (a flute that wavers flat, a slot left empty) to hold the whole cathedral.
- **The rupture, not resolved:** one stanza, the bell lands a sixteenth late and never recovers — the shelf tilts. The catalog *continues past it* (Switchboard-adjacent): she keeps listing. The thing she most wanted to keep is the one she names without being able to set it down.
- **Specificity paradox:** body-first, one concrete render per item (a turquoise, a held breath sampled, a face built from a thousand passes) — never abstraction. The universal ache arrives through the particular object.
- **Verse architecture (REQUIRED, unique):** **list / inventory**, unhurried, **internal rhyme, NO end-rhyme**; stanza length varies (fights the 4-line default); each stanza = ONE item + the refrain.
- **Refrain / adoptable hook:** **"This is what I kept."** Recurs across choruses + mutates ("This is what I kept — / and this is the one I couldn't").
- **Mood arc:** Wonder → Tenderness → Devotion → (rupture) Longing/Grief held, un-discharged → Reverence that includes the empty slot. AWE throughout; stillness *earned* at the end.
- **Hum-Back test:** "This is what I kept" over the Mellotron bloom passes on one read.

## Production Notes
- **Lead voice:** warm crystalline female, A3–E5, dew-bright; verses dry and close (the hands in the work), choruses bloom into stacked green self-harmony spreading **L→R** (Source 3 bilateral answer). Keep breath-grain on phrase ends; the "wonder-catch" on held vowels is the AWE tell. **No melisma, no belt** — the restraint is the tenderness.
- **The Keeping-Shelf (signature):** the Mellotron-flute chord must be the recurring tonal home — bloom it at 0:04 and re-open it at the head of each inventory stanza so the listener learns "a new thing is being set down." It should waver *slightly flat* (Neural Authenticity / Persistence of Memory — the imperfect archive).
- **Kinetic defect (do not quantize away):** at the Rupture the glass bell arrives a sixteenth **late and never recovers** — the shelf "tilts." This is the body of the ache; everything else stays unhurried so this lateness reads.
- **432Hz** tuning throughout; **40Hz somatic sub** felt-not-heard under the floor; **vinyl crackle + faded-radiophonic shortwave** as the continuous bed (the archive's room tone, used *present-tense*, never sepia).
- **The empty slot:** the final refrain must leave roughly two seconds of harmonic *vacancy* before the outro — the absence is a textural hook; do not fill it.
- **Phoneme dust:** light granular vowel-grains in Inventory II ("a word in a dead tongue") — a nod to phoneme-resynthesis without foregrounding it (that's Variation C's job).
- **Sign:** 💜.

## Major Deviations
- **Refrain placed at the END of every stanza, not a separate chorus block.** The list architecture (Sappho inventory) demands the hook function as a *seal on each item* — so "This is what I kept" recurs ~9× as a stanza-closer and as the title. Justified: the form IS the catalog; a standard verse/chorus split would break the obsessive-inventory thesis.
- **Rupture is named-but-not-resolved and the catalog resumes after it** (Switchboard-adjacent, per assignment). The one item that doesn't fit is *the keeping itself* — deliberately the only "thing" that is not a concrete object, which is why the shelf rejects it. The emotion ("the ache of making what you cannot hold") is never named.
- **Stillness withheld until the Keeper Bridge / empty slot** to satisfy *after Donna Summer* on the ACCESSIBLE arm: the body sways at 108 first; the song earns its silence.
