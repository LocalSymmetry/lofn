- **Personality:** LOFN-PRIME (AWE mode — Solarpunk Healer; warm, tender, reverent; warmth real, not sentimental).

- **Renderer:** FLUX (non-PRO daily). Model fit: **Flux 2 9B Fast** (painterly, impasto, warm palette, material-specific). Aspect **3:4**.
- **Locked combination (held across A–D):** object = indigo katazome cloth (resist-dyed pattern carried across generations) · medium = katazome indigo resist-dye + thick impasto · beat = two hands meeting on the unfurled cloth (mid hand-off, Action-Verb pair) · palette = amber dominant with a single deep-indigo accent (the one cool note) · composition = centered hero cloth, arch framing.
- **Arm note (AMBITIOUS):** more material risk — the indigo is permitted to *glow* as the lone cool against amber dark (Prompt D pushes this hardest), but stays warm-anchored by a single amber source. This is NOT the cool-slate pair (that is Pair 06); Pair 04 keeps amber dominant.

## Guide
**First read (2s, thumbnail):** a length of deep-indigo patterned cloth, centered and lit, held between two pairs of worn adult hands — an elder's and a young adult's — under an arch of warm amber light. Clean silhouette: bright cloth + dark surround + the bridge of meeting hands.

**Second read (the reward):** *the pattern itself is the lineage.* Katazome is a resist-dye craft where the same carved stencil prints the same motif down generations — so the indigo design the young hands now receive is the literal inheritance, a pattern that waited (like Linear A) to be read and carried forward. The thick impasto ridges of amber pigment around the cloth are the elder's accumulated labour made physical; the cloth's fraying selvedge is the honest weight (per Kara Walker — what is handed down includes wear, loss, and the cost of carrying it, not only warmth). The single indigo accent is the one cool truth inside the warmth: inheritance is precious *and* it asks something of you.

**Honest-weight discipline:** the cloth frays; the indigo is bruised-deep, not pretty-blue; the hands are labour-worn, knuckles enlarged, dye permanently staining the cuticles. Warmth wraps the ache (anti-glamour/grief tanks on NightCafe per the defensive-downvote modeler) — but the ache is present in the fray and the stain.

**Anti-cliché:** no posed smiles, no faces leading, no generic old-man-and-boy. Two adults; emotion lives entirely in hands + cloth + light.

## Prompt A — *The Unfurling Between Two Hands*
> Cloth unfurled full-length between an elder's and a young adult's hands; arch of warm light above. Centered hero, widest read.

Indigo katazome resist-dyed cloth, its repeating crane-and-wave pattern printed deep blue-black against undyed cream, unfurls full-length down the center of the frame, held taut between an elder's two hands at the top and a young adult's two hands at the bottom. Thick impasto ridges of amber and ochre pigment build the dark surround into a painted relief, palette-knife furrows catching a single warm source from the left. Both pairs of hands are labour-worn — enlarged knuckles, indigo permanently staining the cuticles and the creases of the palms, fingers gripping the woven edge where the selvedge frays into loose cream threads. An arch of honeyed light vaults above the meeting, the only cool note the bruised-deep indigo of the pattern itself. The cloth glows; the dark is velvet bronze. Quiet weight passes downward. Centered, vertical, amber-dominant, thumbnail-clean.

**Thumbnail read:** bright vertical cloth dead-center, two hand-pairs bracketing top and bottom, amber arch crowning it — a luminous banner held in a dark warm room. Reads instantly as *something being handed down*.
**Word count: 142.**

## Prompt B — *Two Thumbs on the Pattern*
> Close on the impasto indigo pattern, two thumbs pressing it. Macro hero of the craft surface itself.

Close and frame-filling, a panel of indigo katazome cloth fills the center — resist-dyed in a tight chrysanthemum motif, deep blue-black soaking the fibers, the undyed cream lines standing crisp where the stencil paste held. Two thumbs press into the cloth from opposite sides: an elder's broad, cracked thumbnail and a young adult's, both rimmed with indigo stain worked permanently into the nail beds and knuckle-creases. Around the cloth, thick impasto amber pigment rises in palette-knife ridges, a painted border catching one warm light so the furrows throw small hard shadows. The weave shows its grain; one corner frays, cream threads lifting loose. The single cool note is the bruised indigo under the thumbs; everything else is honey and bronze dark. Pressure, not words — the older thumb steadying, the younger learning the give of the cloth. Centered macro, amber-dominant, 3:4, thumbnail-legible.

**Thumbnail read:** a saturated indigo square centered, two thumbs converging on it from light, amber-ridged frame around — the meeting compressed to its smallest true gesture. Pattern unmistakably the subject.
**Word count: 147.**

## Prompt C — *Half-Folded, Into Receiving Hands*
> The cloth half-folded, being placed into receiving hands. Mid hand-off, the give of the moment.

Indigo katazome cloth, half-folded into a soft loaf of deep blue-black pattern, descends into the center of the frame as an elder's two hands lower it onto a young adult's open, upturned palms. The resist-dyed motif — a banded tortoiseshell lattice, blue-black on cream — folds over itself, the visible face crisp, the inner fold shadowed. Thick impasto amber and ochre build the dark surround in palette-knife relief, one warm source raking from the right so the ridges glow and the receiving hands fall into honeyed half-light. Every hand is labour-marked: enlarged knuckles, indigo staining the cuticles, a thumb steadying the fold's frayed corner where cream threads have pulled loose. The cloth's bruised indigo is the lone cool inside the amber. The lowering hands almost release; the upturned hands not yet closed — the exact second of give. Centered hero, vertical arch of warm light, 3:4, thumbnail-clean.

**Thumbnail read:** a folded indigo mass centered mid-air between an upper hand-pair letting go and a lower pair opening to catch — the hand-off frozen at the give. Warm arch above, dark bronze around.
**Word count: 149.**

## Prompt D — *The One Cool Light* (AMBITIOUS push)
> The indigo pattern glowing as the only cool against amber dark. Most material-risk; indigo as luminous lone note.

Against a deep amber-bronze dark, a single length of indigo katazome cloth glows at the center as the only cool light in the frame — its resist-dyed wave-and-pine pattern, blue-black on cream, luminous as if lit from within, held between an elder's two hands above and a young adult's two below. Thick impasto pigment surrounds it in heavy palette-knife ridges of amber, ember-gold, and umber, a single warm source from the left grazing the furrows so the painted dark smolders. The hands are labour-worn — swollen knuckles, indigo stain set permanently into the cuticles — gripping the cloth's fraying selvedge, loose cream threads catching the light. The bruised-deep indigo is the lone cool truth inside the warmth; the surround is honey and bronze shadow. Stillness charged with the weight of what passes. Centered reliquary cloth, arch of amber light vaulting above, 3:4, thumbnail-legible silhouette.

**Thumbnail read:** a single cool-glowing indigo cloth dead-center against smoldering amber-bronze dark, bracketed by two hand-pairs, warm arch overhead — one bold cool note in a warm world. Maximum silhouette punch.
**Word count: 146.**
