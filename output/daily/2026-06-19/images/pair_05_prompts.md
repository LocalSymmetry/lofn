## Guide
**Container Test (this pair):** the jar is a small world. Keep the **silhouette clean and iconic** — one rounded glass vessel, instantly read on a dark chair — and **compress everything around it** (the chair, the room, the support) into mezzotint shadow. Then **pack the INTERIOR with impossible warm abundance**: a tiny inherited orchard, terraced fields, a thread of smoke from a hearth no bigger than a fingernail. The glass is the membrane between a stripped dark world and a dense glowing one.

**First read (2s):** a glowing seed jar on a worn, empty chair, decentered, the rest of the frame velvet dark. **Second read:** the warmth is a *whole small world growing inside the glass* — the inheritance left for someone not yet in the room — and the empty chair says the one who left it is **gone**. Absence shown, never named: the dent in the cushion, the chair turned slightly away, the single burnished light belonging only to the jar's interior.

**Honest-weight (Walker friction):** the jar sits on a *worn, repaired* chair, the seat sagged from a life of sitting; the warmth is bequeathed, not free — someone is no longer here to hold it.

## Prompt A — jar on the worn chair, a tiny amber orchard glowing inside
A glass seed jar rests on the cracked leather seat of an empty wooden chair, rendered in mezzotint velvet — deep burnished blacks, one warm light belonging only to the jar's interior. Inside the clean rounded glass grows a tiny amber orchard: rows of miniature trees heavy with honey-gold fruit, a sepia-bronze field rolling to a horizon no wider than a thumb, a thread of smoke rising from a hearth the size of a seed. The jar's silhouette stays simple and iconic against the dark; the chair, worn and sagged where a body used to sit, dissolves into shadow. Warm sepia-bronze light pools on the leather, leaving the room beyond in velvet engraved dark. The seat is empty, slightly turned away. A whole inherited world waits on the chair of someone now gone.

**Thumbnail read:** a glowing jar on a dark, empty chair, decentered — instantly legible; the warm point reads as "something living was left here."

## Prompt B — close on the jar's interior world: terraced fields, a thread of smoke
Miniature terraced fields and a thread of woodsmoke fill the inside of a glass seed jar, seen close, the vessel held in mezzotint velvet darkness with one burnished warm light glowing from within the glass. Tiny sepia-bronze terraces step down an interior hillside, each level dense with amber grain, a ribbon of river catching honey-gold, a single thread of smoke curling from a hearth no larger than a grain of wheat. The curved glass bends the warmth into a soft burnished halo; condensation beads the inner wall like dew on a world's own morning. The jar's outline stays round and clean against engraved black; behind it the worn edge of an empty chair sinks into shadow. A small inherited country breathes inside the glass, left behind, still warm, waiting for hands that have not yet arrived.

**Thumbnail read:** a luminous amber sphere in the dark — reads instantly as a glowing vessel; second-read reveals a whole tiny landscape inside.

## Prompt C — the empty chair, the jar a warm point decentered
An empty wooden chair stands in mezzotint velvet shadow, its worn seat and turned spindles barely surfacing from engraved black, while a glass seed jar glows as a single warm point set well off-center on the cushion. Inside the clean rounded glass a small living world burns sepia-bronze: a fingernail orchard, amber fields, a hearth-thread of smoke, the only light in the frame. The chair holds the dent of a long-gone body; a folded indigo cloth slumps over one arm, its pattern lost to the dark. Warm bronze light falls only on the jar and the leather beneath it, the rest of the room dissolving into deep burnished black. The composition leans into the shadow, the lit jar small against the weight of empty space. Inheritance left on a chair no one fills now.

**Thumbnail read:** a vast dark frame with one small warm jar low and to the side — the emptiness reads first, the glowing inheritance second.

## Prompt D — a young adult's hand just entering frame to lift it
A young adult's hand enters the frame from the shadow, fingers reaching for a glass seed jar that glows on the cracked seat of an empty chair, the whole scene engraved in mezzotint velvet — deep burnished blacks, one warm light from the jar's interior. Inside the clean rounded glass a tiny amber orchard and terraced sepia-bronze fields burn, a thread of smoke rising from a hearth the size of a seed. The hand is bare, the knuckles work-worn, the gesture careful, not yet touching — the warmth of the small inherited world catches the fingertips first. The chair sags where someone used to sit and sits no longer; warm bronze light pools on the leather and the reaching hand, the room beyond lost to velvet dark. The decentered jar waits to be lifted by the one who comes after.

**Thumbnail read:** a glowing jar on a dark chair with a hand reaching in from the edge — the mid-gesture lift reads instantly; the empty seat says who is gone.

## Pair Best
1. **Prompt D** — *a young adult's hand just entering frame to lift it.* Strongest pair: it satisfies the Action-Verb hand-off beat while keeping the empty-chair absence, the cleanest emotional second-read (warmth meeting the new hand first), and the most legible thumbnail story (jar + reaching hand on a dark seat). Highest NightCafe legibility-plus-ache balance.
2. **Prompt A** — *jar on the worn chair, tiny amber orchard.* The purest statement of the locked combination: iconic jar silhouette, densified interior orchard, empty turned-away seat. Best "object left behind" stillness; safest thumbnail.

(B is the richest second-read but a riskier first-read — the chair nearly vanishes; C is the most honest-weight/absence but the dimmest thumbnail. Lead with D and A.)
