## Guide
**First read (2s):** a lone old craftsman low in the frame, a small bright fork in his hands, a warm room glowing empty above him — a held, listening hush.
**Second read:** the fork is a *pitch* — the exact note a father-figure tuned a whole life to, now humming on the edge of being handed over. The beat is *anticipation*: the gift is not yet given. His listening posture and hovering hand carry the tenderness; the warm negative space above is the silence the note will travel into. Honest-weight (Walker): the brass is worn thin from being held — inheritance costs the holder before it reaches anyone. Title-as-frame: "The Fork That Holds the Note" tells the voter this small object keeps a sound alive across years.

## Prompt A
*Elder seated low-frame, fork held to the candlelight, examining the pitch he is about to give away.*

> Thick smeared oil paint, worked with a knuckled palette knife, builds an old craftsman seated low in the frame, a worn brass tuning fork held up into a single candle flame. Ridged ochre and honey-amber strokes drag across his lined face and the loose collar at his throat; the room above him opens into warm empty negative space, the paint thinning to a glowing wash near the top. His thumb rests against the cold metal, testing it, his gaze fixed on the fork as a man studies a thing he is about to give away and cannot yet. Worn brass gone soft-gold at the grip, candle-warm canvas grain showing through every smear. Candlelit ochre dominant, one warm source low and to the side, deep worked shadow behind. A held breath before the note. Aspect 3:4.

**Thumbnail read:** dark seated mass, bottom third; one bright vertical fork-and-flame; warm glow filling the top — a lone figure cradling a small light. Reads instantly as *old man, small luminous object, hush.*

## Prompt B
*The fork still humming, his eyes closed, listening to the inherited note one last time before it leaves his hands.*

> Smeared candlelit pigment, thick and dragged, shapes a lone elder low in the frame with a struck tuning fork lifted near his temple, its brass still trembling. Ochre and worn-gold strokes smear across his closed eyelids and the deep creases at his mouth; warm negative space spreads through the upper canvas, the paint loosening to a honeyed haze. His shoulders settle, his head tilts a degree toward the metal, his free hand open and motionless on his knee — a body holding still to hear a sound only he knows the meaning of. Brass rubbed pale at the tines, candle-warm grain breaking through every worked ridge. Candlelit ochre throughout, a single warm flame off-frame, velvet-dark behind his back. The last private listening before the pitch is passed. Aspect 3:4.

**Thumbnail read:** low dark silhouette, head-tilt unmistakable; a thin bright fork at the temple; warm empty room above — reads as *a man listening to something small and precious.*

Ridged honey and amber paint drags across the rumpled cloth and his outstretched knuckles; the upper frame holds warm empty negative space, pigment thinning to a glowing ground. His fingers curl in the air a finger-width from the fork, not yet closing — the hesitation of a man about to lift a thing he means to hand away. Brass tarnished gold and fork-cold against fraying weave, candle-warm canvas tooth in every smear. Candlelit ochre dominant, one low warm source raking the cloth, worked shadow pooling beneath the table. The pause before the hand commits. Aspect 3:4.

**Thumbnail read:** horizontal worn cloth, bright small fork dead-center-low, hovering hand reaching in; dark figure behind, warm glow above — reads as *an object on a table about to be picked up.*

## Prompt D

> Smeared painterly pigment, thick and palette-dragged, frames a worn brass tuning fork held up against a warm glowing window, its dark twin-tined silhouette crisp on the amber light, an old craftsman seated low in the lower third beneath it. Ochre and honey strokes smear the window's glow and the soft edge of his bowed head and shoulder; the bright pane fills the upper frame as warm negative space, paint loosening toward a luminous haze. His arm lifts the fork steady into the light while the rest of him stays sunk in worked shadow — the small inherited shape made larger than the man who has carried it. Brass gone warm-gold at the grip, candlelit grain showing through every ridge. Candlelit ochre dominant, the window the single warm source, velvet dark around the figure. The note raised before it is released. Aspect 3:4.

**Thumbnail read:** glowing window-rectangle up top, hard black fork-silhouette against it, small dark figure clinging to the lower edge — reads instantly even at smallest size as *a small fork held up to the light.*

## Pair Best
1. **Prompt B — "the fork still humming, eyes closed, listening."** Strongest emotional second-read: the closed-eye head-tilt *is* the anticipation made visible, and "the last private listening before the pitch is passed" lands the not-yet-given beat without naming it. Cleanest thumbnail silhouette (head-tilt + bright fork at temple). Most honest-weight — the holder pays in private before anyone receives.
2. **Prompt D — fork silhouette against the warm window.** Best pure thumbnail legibility (hard black fork on a glowing pane survives any downscale) and the boldest single choice (object made larger than the man). Slightly less tender than B, so ranked second.

Prompts A and C are strong supports: A is the most classically warm/Rembrandt-paternal; C carries the literal hand-hover gesture (useful if the Action-Verb rule wants visible motion in this pair).
