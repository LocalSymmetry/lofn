## Guide
First read (2 seconds): a warm-lit person holds a glowing gold-veined bowl out of velvet-black dark — tenderness without a smile, the feeling carried in the hands and the held breath.
**Second read = the cracks ARE the inheritance.** The bowl was broken and mended in gold; every seam is a fracture made permanent and golden — what's handed down includes the breaking, not only the warmth. The elder who repaired it is gone from the frame, present only as reflection or as the gold he left behind. Honest weight (Walker): the gift is also a wound, healed in gold and now passed on.

## Prompt A — ¾ portrait cupping the bowl
A young adult cups a kintsugi repair bowl in both hands, the gold-leaf restoration seams catching a single warm source against velvet black. Crackled celadon glaze, dull at the rim, is laced with thick rivers of gold where the bowl once broke and was mended, every fracture turned to a bright vein. ¾ portrait, shoulders angled, the bowl held forward in the foreground and hero-lit so it burns amber while the face stays half in honeyed shadow. The hands are worn-knuckled, careful, just-closed around the weight; the mouth is steady, the eyes lowered to the gold as if reading it. No smile — only the stillness of a thing just received. Ember-gold dominant, velvet-black surround, the glaze warm with reflected fire. A portrait of inheritance held, the breaking made golden in the hands. Aspect 3:4.
*Thumbnail read: a glowing gold-veined bowl cupped against black, a lowered face above it — legible warmth at a glance.*

## Prompt B — the bowl alone, hero-lit, two hands entering
A kintsugi repair bowl rests on dark folded cloth, hero-lit by one warm source, its gold-leaf restoration seams the brightest thing in the velvet-black frame. The crackled stoneware is matte and ember-brown, split by broad gold rivers where it was shattered and rejoined, the mend wider and more golden than the breaks it covers. Two worn adult hands enter low from the frame's edge, fingertips just reaching the rim, not yet lifting — the moment before the weight transfers. Reliquary stillness, the bowl centered and luminous, every seam catching amber. The cloth beneath drinks the light to near-black. No figure, no face, only the object and the entering hands; the emotion lives in the careful, unhurried reach. Ember-gold on velvet black, single warm source, the gold veins glowing like banked coals. A portrait of what is about to be handed down. Aspect 3:4.
*Thumbnail read: a bright gold-laced bowl glowing center-frame on black with hands creeping in — reliquary hero shot.*

## Prompt C — the elder reflected in the bowl's glaze
A young adult holds a kintsugi repair bowl close, and in its dark gold-glazed curve the warm reflection of an elder's face surfaces, small and amber, the only place he still appears. Gold-leaf restoration seams thread the crackled black glaze, each mended fracture a vein of light, and the curved interior catches a single warm source so the bowl glows like a held ember against velvet black. ¾ portrait, the bowl lifted into the foreground and hero-lit; the holder's own face stays in honeyed shadow above, eyes down on the reflection. Worn hands brace the bowl steady, careful of the weight just received. No smile — recognition, quiet, the elder present only as a warm ghost in the glaze. Ember-gold dominant, velvet-black surround, second face faint in the gold. A portrait of lineage seen in the mend itself. Aspect 3:4.
*Thumbnail read: a glowing gold-veined bowl with a small amber face caught in its curve, held against black — second-read reward built in.*

## Prompt D — the gold seams catching the only light
A kintsugi repair bowl tilts in a young adult's hands so its gold-leaf restoration seams catch the single warm source and become the only bright lines in an almost black frame. The crackled glaze itself reads as deep ember-brown, nearly lost to the velvet dark, but the mended fractures blaze — a branching map of gold where the bowl was broken and rejoined, the lineage drawn in light across the curve. ¾ portrait, bowl held forward and low, the figure a warm silhouette behind it, face barely modeled in amber shadow. Worn fingers cradle the underside, steady and reverent with the weight. No smile, no flourish — only the seams glowing in held stillness. Ember-gold veins on velvet black, one warm source, the gold the sole focal light. A portrait of the cracks themselves, the inheritance written in gold. Aspect 3:4.
*Thumbnail read: a black bowl webbed with blazing gold seams suspended in dark — the cracks read as pure light at small size.*
