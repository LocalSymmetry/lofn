## Prompt A — the instant of contact
Two adult hands meet on a small incised clay tablet rendered in cracked egg tempera, the crazed matte paint webbing fine pale veins across honey-amber skin and gold-warm clay. An elder's worn hand, knuckles thick and tendons raised, presses the tablet from below; a younger adult's steadier hand closes over it from above, fingertips just touching the older thumb. The clay carries rows of shallow Minoan-like incised marks and glows as the single warm source, throwing amber up into both palms and leaving the surround a deep velvet brown. Fresco-fine cracks run through the dark ground and across the tablet's edge. The pressure where the two hands overlap holds the whole exchange — patient, unhurried, the weight of a long craft set down into new fingers without a word. Close two-hands macro, honey-amber on velvet dark.

*Why it wins / thumbnail read:* the overlap of worn + younger hand is the clearest possible "hand-off" silhouette, glowing tablet dead-center.

## Prompt B — the elder's worn hand alone laying it down
An elder's single worn hand lowers a small incised clay tablet onto a dark surface, painted in cracked egg tempera so a crazed pale web runs over honey-amber skin, swollen knuckles, and gold-warm clay alike. The hand is alone in frame, fingers spread to release, a thumb still bearing the tablet's weight; the younger hand has not yet arrived. Shallow Minoan-like marks cut the clay in even rows, and the tablet glows as the only warm source, pooling amber under the curled fingers and sinking the surround into velvet brown-black. Hairline tempera cracks thread the matte dark ground. The slight tremor in the laying-down — the way the old fingers linger a half-second before they let go — carries a craft handed off after a lifetime. Close hand-and-object macro, honey-amber against deep dark.

*Why it wins / thumbnail read:* the lone releasing hand over a glowing tablet reads as "giving" at thumbnail; the lingering fingers carry honest weight.

## Prompt C — the young hand just closing on it
A younger adult's hand closes around a small incised clay tablet just set into its palm, rendered in cracked egg tempera with a crazed matte web of pale cracks over warm skin and gold-toned clay. The hand is alone in frame, fingers curling inward to take the weight, the tablet settling against the heel of the palm. Rows of shallow Minoan-like marks score the honey-amber clay, which glows as the single warm source and casts amber along each tendon while the surround falls to velvet dark. Fine fresco cracks split the matte black ground behind. The careful tightening of the grip — fingers learning the heft of a thing far older than the hand that now holds it — carries the stillness of something just received and not yet understood. Close hand-and-object macro, honey-amber on velvet black.

*Why it wins / thumbnail read:* the curling young fingers around the lit slab read instantly as "just received"; quiet inheritance, Wyeth-still.

## Prompt D — both hands + a faint incised glow spelling one mark
Two adult hands cradle a small incised clay tablet together, painted in cracked egg tempera whose crazed matte web laces honey-amber skin and gold-warm clay. An elder's worn hand cups it from beneath, a younger adult's hand steadies its upper edge, fingers interlaced along the rim. Rows of shallow Minoan-like marks cover the clay, and within them a single mark glows brighter than the rest — a faint amber line of light tracing one incised sign as though a 3,500-year-old word had just been read aloud. The tablet is the only warm source, lifting amber into both sets of knuckles and dropping the surround to velvet brown-black. Hairline tempera cracks run through clay and dark ground. The shared hold is even and unhurried, weight balanced between old hands and new. Close two-hands macro, honey-amber on velvet dark.

*Why it wins / thumbnail read:* both hands cradling a tablet with one brighter glowing mark is the richest second-read — the tongue literally lighting up as it is finally heard.

## Pair Best
1. **Prompt A — the instant of contact** *(strongest)*: the overlapping worn-and-younger hands give the cleanest hand-off silhouette and satisfy the Action-Verb (mid-gesture) rule most directly; warmest, most legible in 2s.
2. **Prompt D — the faint glowing mark** *(strong second)*: best second-read reward (one Minoan sign lighting as the dead tongue is read); richest payoff for the Linear-A thread, slightly busier at thumbnail than A.
B and C are excellent quieter variants (single-hand giving / single-hand receiving) but each shows only one side of the exchange, so they rank below the two two-hand prompts.
