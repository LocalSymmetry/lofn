## Guide — why the cool palette is earned, and how the one amber core keeps it votable
- **The barbell needs one cool pair.** Five pairs lead honey-warm; this AMBITIOUS pair earns contrast by going cool slate-grey dominant — a *cold* forge — so the whole daily set is not one temperature. Cool reads as honest here: the forge is cold because its keeper has gone; the warmth has nearly run out.
- **The single amber core is the safety rail.** NightCafe votes warm in under 2 seconds, and a fully cool image risks the defensive downvote. So this pair keeps exactly ONE legible amber source — the last surviving coal (A, C, D) or the warm-worn handle itself (B) — bright and centered enough that the thumbnail still reads *warm-centered*. The cool slate is the surround; the amber is the heart. Remove that one core and the pair fails the gate.
- **Focal hierarchy:** (1) the one amber core — coal or warm-worn grip — as the sole warm light; (2) the worn smith's hand-tool it lights; (3) the young adult's silhouette / lone receiving hand; (4) the worn oak grain as the reward read.
- **Medium constant:** thick impasto — slate-grey paint troweled in heavy ridged palette-knife strokes; cold steel glints on the impasto crests, faint ember-amber pooling in the valleys.
- **Object constant:** a worn smith's hammer (the father-figure's decades-old tool), iron head dulled, oak handle rubbed pale and warm where one grip wore it smooth.
- **Beat constant:** the young adult ALONE — first receiving / first holding the tool at the cold forge with one last warm coal. No second figure; the keeper is gone.
- **Palette constant:** cool slate-grey / ash-blue dominant + ONE amber core as the single warm source + cold blue-grey shadow surround.
- **The second-read = the worn handle:** the oak burnished pale by a lifetime of one repeated hold reads, up close, as the labour and the lineage — weight and warmth together, the Walker-honesty inside the warmth.
- **Thumbnail test:** at small size the silhouette is unmistakably a lone figure (or a single tool) lit by one warm core against cold grey.
