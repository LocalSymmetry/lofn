## ⚠️ THEME COMPLIANCE CHECK *(complete before writing anything else)*

> This is the first gate. Fail it and the best prompt in the world scores 4 likes.

**Step 1 — Parse the brief literally:**
Write the theme as a noun phrase AND check for action verbs.
- Theme noun: {{e.g. "astronauts"}}
- Action modifier: {{e.g. "at work" | "building" | "exploring" | none}}

**Step 2 — Action verb test:**
Does the theme contain or imply an action verb (at work, building, fighting, exploring, creating, dancing, etc.)?

- **YES → ACTION BRIEF.** Portrait mode is DISQUALIFIED unless the action reads on the face alone. Required: multiple figures OR clear mid-task action frozen in frame OR environmental scale showing the work happening NOW. Cinematic wide or medium shot. Format: landscape or wide portrait.
  - *Lesson: NightCafe #1275 "Astronauts at Work" — our single portrait cave painting scored 4 likes / 3.27 rating. Winner had 791 likes: 4+ figures, epic Mars landscape, machinery, action everywhere. "At work" = crew + environment + action. Portrait = contemplation. Wrong mode entirely.*

- **NO → SUBJECT BRIEF.** Portrait-first composition is permitted. Proceed to structural axes.

**Step 3 — Format lock:**
Based on Step 2, lock the format BEFORE choosing any axis:
- Action brief → landscape or wide-format cinematic (16:9 or 3:2). Multiple figures default.
- Subject brief → portrait (3:4) or square (1:1). Single figure default.

> ⚠️ **MANDATORY STRUCTURAL AXES — Always include these two. They are not aesthetic choices; they are engagement architecture. Data from 80+ NightCafe posts confirms these are the highest-leverage structural decisions.**

### AXIS S1 — COMPOSITION STRUCTURE *(mandatory)*
*Controls how the image organizes space at the macro level. Choose ONE per concept.*
- **Macro isolation** — Subject fills 70–80% of frame. No environment competes. Highest engagement ceiling (311-like golden rose, 238-like koi). Use when subject has intrinsic texture/detail worth zooming into.
- **Bilateral symmetry + mirror axis** — Vertical subject with horizontal spread (wings, water reflection, arms). Mirror axis doubles the subject vertically. Yin-yang, reflection in water, symmetric wingspread. Reliable 20–50 likes.
- **Scale contrast** — Giant object or creature + tiny human silhouette below/beside = instant depth read. Jellyfish forest, goddess above mortal. Reliable 16–20 likes.
- **Portrait crown** — Close-crop portrait, face 60%+ of frame, ornamental elements arching behind as crown geometry. The proven 87–150 like range for facial subjects.
- **Architectural fill** — Full body ONLY when the surrounding structure (wings, roots, arches, mechanical elements) fills edge-to-edge, leaving no dead space. Steampunk fairy rule.

### AXIS S2 — LIGHT ARCHITECTURE *(mandatory)*
*Controls the single dominant light source. Choose ONE per concept. Diffuse ambient = 7 likes.*
- **Internal glow** — Light emanates FROM the subject outward. Subject glows against dark surround. The 311-like rose, 87-like fairy, 72-like gold dissolution. Maximum contrast, maximum engagement.
- **Backlit silhouette** — Strong warm source (sunset, moon, portal) BEHIND the subject. Subject partially silhouetted with rim light. 33-like goddess. High drama.
- **Below-left candle/flame** — Single warm source below and to the left, as in 17th-century oil painting tradition. Rembrandtian shadow-fall on right side of face. 13–25 likes for portrait subjects.
- **Bioluminescent field** — Multiple small cool-warm light sources (fungi, jellyfish, fireflies) distributed through the frame creating a constellation of light. 16–22 likes.
- **Reflective surface** — Light bounces up from water, mirror, or wet ground beneath the subject. Creates dual directionality (above + below). Pairs with mirror axis.
