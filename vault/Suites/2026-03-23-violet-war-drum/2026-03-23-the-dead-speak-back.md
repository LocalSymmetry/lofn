[RAW GENERATION PROMPT / STEP 08]
Spectral ritual electronic track with hidden 140 BPM pulse in A minor, opening disguised as harmonic static that slowly reveals heartbeat and throat overtones, female voice emerging from noise into personhood, ghost choir fragments and whispered names, chorus repeating “The noise was singing,” bridge where names and harmonics bloom together while pulse holds, uncanny but constant intensity, no sentimental ghost-story cues.

[ARTIST-REFINED PROMPT / STEP 09]
The drama is not in a jump scare but in recognition. From the first second the dead are already present; the song simply teaches the ear how to hear them. Let the arrangement peel static into body, hiss into vowel, noise into witness. The lead begins as almost-texture and ends as a plural speaking force. It should feel eerie, irreversible, and strangely plainspoken.

[FINAL SUNO / UDIO PROMPT / STEP 10]
Spectral electronic / ritual nocturne at hidden 140 BPM, A minor. Start with static that is already harmonic and rhythmically alive beneath the surface. Gradually reveal buried pulse, throat overtones, ghost fragments, and a female lead resolving from noise into personhood. Lyrics should focus on noticing patterns, hearing names in static, and realizing the dead were present all along. Chorus is simple and haunting: “The noise was singing.” Bridge lets names and harmonics bloom together without dropping intensity. No ambient drift, no melodramatic ghost tropes—uncanny clarity, constant pressure.
