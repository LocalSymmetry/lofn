[RAW GENERATION PROMPT / STEP 08]
Experimental industrial bass track at 140 BPM in C minor with hollowed-out mids, huge 30Hz sub foundation, glassy upper static, female vocal split into low rumble copy and frozen high shard, verses describe invisible force and bodily damage, chorus repeats “Feel the violet mark,” bridge removes mids almost entirely while pulse and high dust remain, no warm bloom, no full-spectrum comfort.

[ARTIST-REFINED PROMPT / STEP 09]
Design it like a wound in the frequency spectrum. The low end should feel like an unseen fist, the top end like ultraviolet glass dust, and the missing middle should make the listener mentally reconstruct the absent body. The writing is sensory, stripped down, and physical rather than essayistic. When the voice briefly recombines, it should feel costly and intimate.

[FINAL SUNO / UDIO PROMPT / STEP 10]
Experimental bass / industrial techno at 140 BPM, C minor. Instant low-frequency impact with crystalline static above and a deliberately hollow midrange. Female lead split into two impossible bodies: subterranean rumble and pain-threshold spectral high layer. Minimal, sharp lyrics about pressure, hidden damage, and bodily testimony. Chorus must be chantable: “Feel the violet mark.” Bridge strips away familiar mids so only pulse and glass-dust highs remain. No warmth, no obvious dubstep theatrics, no horror cliches—make it physically wrong and addictive.

[PRODUCTION NOTES]
- Emphasize the asymmetry: massive sub, brittle highs, restrained mids.
- Keep kick mostly living in the lowest register for bodily feel.
- Vocal split should be obvious in verses and partially recombined in chorus/final lift.
- Bridge should feel uncanny through subtraction, not softness.
- Best result comes from sound-design discipline rather than maximal arrangement density.
