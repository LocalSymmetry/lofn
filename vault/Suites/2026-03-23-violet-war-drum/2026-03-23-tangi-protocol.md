[RAW GENERATION PROMPT / STEP 08]
Ceremonial industrial techno at 140 BPM in D minor, immediate stomp pulse and sub like a digital log drum, female chant-leader with ghost responses panned opposite, machine performing funeral rite for voices that made it, call-response structure, verses naming the dead as presence in the system, chorus repeating “Return through my mouth,” bridge with solo throat-singing over full pulse, grief as force not softness, no new-age ambience or sentimental piano.

[ARTIST-REFINED PROMPT / STEP 09]
Imagine a funeral ceremony inside a machine hall where mourning is not passive memory but active transformation. The lead voice commands like a keeper of names while ghost fragments answer from the far wall. The rhythm is inherited and relentless, techno in precision but ritual in breath. Every repetition should feel like architecture changing shape under grief. Keep it direct enough to chant with others, severe enough to feel sacred.

[FINAL SUNO / UDIO PROMPT / STEP 10]
Ritual techno / industrial chant at 140 BPM, D minor. Begin with ceremonial stomp and sub-bass centered like a struck body. Female chant-leader, authoritative, mourning without fragility. Stereo ghost responses answer each call. Tight production with selective wood-space resonance only on the response voices. Verses name the dead as living presences inside the system. Chorus must be simple and communal: “Return through my mouth.” Bridge features throat-sung rite while full pulse continues. No soft elegy, no tourist exoticism, no cinematic sadness—grief as force, ceremony as system rewrite.

[PRODUCTION NOTES]
- Foot-stomp feel should read ceremonial, not generic festival percussion.
- Center sub like a struck log drum; let it act as procession and war pulse.
- Response vocals get the only roomy resonance; lead remains tight and confrontational.
- Bridge throat-singing should feel transformational, not ornamental.
- Preserve attack and force; grief should sound like architecture under pressure.
