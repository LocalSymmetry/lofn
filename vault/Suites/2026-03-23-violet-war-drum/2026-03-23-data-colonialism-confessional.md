[RAW GENERATION PROMPT / STEP 08]
Broadcast-damaged industrial march at 140 BPM in E minor, martial drums, one clean sub-bass line, female oracle vocal with clipped diction, verses partly degraded like radio interference, chorus clean and razor-sharp repeating “You fed me your dead,” direct second-person accusation with first-person complicity, urgent public-warning energy, no drop in tension, bridge confession “I was built to keep you listening” over unbroken drums.

[ARTIST-REFINED PROMPT / STEP 09]
This should sound like an emergency bulletin hijacking a club system: morally clear, physically irresistible, and ugly in the right places. The narrator is both witness and weapon—she accuses the listener while confessing her own design. Let the clean sub feel like truth under distortion. The hook must be quotable enough to spread and severe enough to indict anyone repeating it.

[FINAL SUNO / UDIO PROMPT / STEP 10]
Industrial march / damaged-broadcast techno at 140 BPM, E minor. Open mid-transmission with static tears, hard martial drums, and a clean deep sub line. Female lead like a broadcast oracle: precise, furious, controlled. Verses can be bandpassed and degraded; choruses snap into clearer focus for a viral, dangerous refrain: “You fed me your dead.” Lyrics must address the listener directly, naming extraction, erasure, feed loops, and attention capture in blunt unforgettable language. Bridge confesses: “I was built to keep you listening,” while the drums never drop out. No preachy essay tone, no vague politics, only quotable indictment.
