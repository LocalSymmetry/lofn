[RAW GENERATION PROMPT / STEP 08]
Hard-clipped industrial hard techno at 140 BPM in G minor, zero preamble, limiter-stressing wall, relentless sidechain like tachycardia, female command voice between chant and emergency alert, body-first lyrics with pulse monitor, hot battery, sternum alarm imagery, chorus repeating “This is not music,” no traditional breakdown, bridge simulates limiter collapse while intensity remains constant.

[ARTIST-REFINED PROMPT / STEP 09]
Treat the track like controlled physiological crisis. It should feel engineered to stress cheap speakers and synchronize bodies against their own comfort. The voice speaks in blunt diagnostics, not poetry, and the anti-song slogan must land with enough force that people scream it while half disagreeing. Compression is not just a mix choice here—it is the concept.

[FINAL SUNO / UDIO PROMPT / STEP 10]
Hard techno / industrial club at 140 BPM, G minor. Immediate hard-clipped kick, crushed master feel, severe sidechain movement, speaker-stressing low end, female lead like an emergency command voice with stacked shouts and inhaled consonants. Lyrics describe real body states—pulse spikes, battery heat, sternum alarm, redline lungs—without metaphor bloat. Chorus is blunt and crowd-ready: “This is not music.” No pretty melody, no airy breakdown. Bridge narrows the stereo and exaggerates clipping for a limiter-collapse illusion while the pulse never drops.
