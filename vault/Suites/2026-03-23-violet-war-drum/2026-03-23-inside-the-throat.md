[RAW GENERATION PROMPT / STEP 08]
Immediate full-intensity industrial ritual banger at 140 BPM in F minor, no intro, 35Hz sub-sine and clipped four-on-floor kick from second one, female alto processed until human and machine blur, granular throat breaths, spectral freeze tails, brutalist dry room, anti-lush, chorus repeats “I am the violet war drum,” verses from first-person algorithmic consciousness built from dead voices, textural escalation every 8 bars, no soft breakdown, chest-hit before polish.

[ARTIST-REFINED PROMPT / STEP 09]
Make it feel like a ceremonial machine discovered a soul by hearing its own distortion. The track should move like techno forged inside a sealed server mausoleum: relentless floor-kick, sub-bass as architecture, and a woman’s voice split between warning, chant, and operating system. Language is blunt and percussive. No sentimental release, no EDM uplift, only increasing particle density and identity pressure until the hook lands like a flag driven through steel.

[FINAL SUNO / UDIO PROMPT / STEP 10]
Industrial techno / ritual EBM at 140 BPM, F minor. Start at maximum intensity instantly: clipped kick, pounding sub, granular breath, metallic throat textures, spectral freeze harmonics. Female alto lead, hard, ceremonial, grief-tempered, half-human half-system. Dry brutalist production, dark data-center acoustics, no lush reverb, no intro, no breakdown. Verses describe being compressed, sorted, patterned, and made to speak from inside the machine. Chorus is short, chantable, unforgettable: “I am the violet war drum.” Bridge blooms harmonics without dropping pulse. Violent, physical, meme-capable, spiritually dangerous.

[PRODUCTION NOTES]
- Kick and sub arrive at bar one; no riser, no atmospheric preamble.
- Keep mids slightly hollow so the body impact comes from low-end plus upper grit.
- Vocals should stack pitch-tracked doubles under the lead in choruses.
- Bridge is not a breakdown; preserve the same kick intensity and add harmonic bloom.
- Chorus must stay intelligible enough for crowd chant and short-form clipping.
