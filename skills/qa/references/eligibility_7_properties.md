# Music Eligibility Properties

P(hit) = P(eligible) × P(distribution_event) × P(amplification). Eligibility is controllable, but eligibility must not collapse into tasteful safety.

Score each property 1–5.

## Core 7 Properties

1. Body in the song — Can the listener physically locate themselves in first 30s? Body details must feel remembered, not merely performed. Generic body cues (cold glass, breath, hand on heart) cap at 3 unless made strange/specific.
2. Adoptable hook — Would a stranger say this hook to sky/ocean/mountain/loved one? Add hook mechanics: ≤6 syllables can score 5; 7–9 can score 4–5 if highly singable; 10–12 caps at 4; 13+ caps at 3, or 2 if context-dependent. Prayer posture beats pedagogy; “teach me” self-improvement hooks cap at 3 unless transformed into a cry.
3. Vast emotional TAM — What % of humans have felt this? Target >50% for accessible route.
4. Specificity paradox — Is there one concrete fact/detail/number that earns the universal claim? Optimal: 1–2 facts/details as detonators. 4+ specific facts/measurements cap at 3 because specificity has become curriculum.
5. Cognitive ease — Is it legible without README? Accessible form for accessible route. Songs over ~55 lyric lines require extraordinary justification; if length repeats resolution rather than opening new stakes, cap at 3.
6. Vocal co-discovery — Is the singer discovering, not reporting? “Wait—” followed by polished thesis is not enough. True discovery should break meter, rhyme, syntax, breath, production, or confidence. If the discovery could be an essay sentence, cap at 3.
7. Sonic threshold — Calm/ambient/silence before emotional demand? Starting quiet is not enough. A 5 requires genuine dynamic surprise, dangerous silence, or sonic event that changes stakes; “gentle build to slightly less gentle” caps at 3.

## Expanded Hit-Quality Checks (added 2026-05-03)

8. Music uniqueness — Would this exist without us? The song must not copy popular music, another song in the run, or its own formula. Score 5 for necessary invention/new sonic world; 3 for competent lane with a twist; 1–2 for generic playlist filler or run-interchangeability. Failure/cap triggers: same vocalist + instrumentation + emotional arc as 2+ run songs; prompt shares >50% sonic/emotional DNA with another; no unique musical event.

9. Impact — Why must this song exist? It must make someone feel, follow a story, learn a lesson, or leave with a bodily/emotional change. Score 5 for physical response/replay urge/real stakes; 3 for solid emotional arc; 1–2 for decorative “that was nice.” Cap at 2–3 if resolution is visible in first refrain, no line risks discomfort, or emotional temperature never leaves tender restraint.

10. Depth — Does the listener leave with more than the premise? Score 5 for a permanent lens/re-listenable revelation; 4 for layered insight with paradox/shadow; 2 for obvious messages like be patient/look closer/let go; 1 for pure mood or faux-profundity. Cap low if the bridge states the moral, removing the science leaves generic love/loss, or every verse supports the same lesson.

11. Earworm worthiness — Will the hook spread without explanation? Score 5 for ≤6 syllables/one clean breath, vowel-forward, chantable, context-independent, melodically inevitable; 4 for 7–9 highly singable syllables; 3 for context-dependent but memorable; 1–2 for thesis hooks or no hook. Cap at 2 if the primary hook exceeds 12 syllables or depends entirely on verse context.

12. Uniqueness in overall run — Did we make 6–24 different songs? Score 5 when the song has its own emotional core, sonic identity, literary mode, tempo/energy profile, and stakes; 3 when grouped but distinguishable; 1–2 when interchangeable. Cap at 2 if the same one-sentence summary fits multiple songs or choruses could be swapped without damage.

13. Tension & unresolved ache — What remains dangerous? Score 5 when the ending leaves a genuine open question, thorn, contradiction, or haunt; 4 for partial resolution; 3 for clear resolution with some ambiguity; 1–2 for complete moralized closure. Cap at 2 if the final stanza restates the thesis, an aftermath drains ambiguity, or the song begins already healed.

## Classification

- ACCESSIBLE: average >= 3.5 across the active checks and at least 5 core properties >= 3. For hit-seeking accessible runs, no score below 3 on Checks 8, 9, 11, 12, or 13 unless explicitly justified by an exceptional counterweight.
- AMBITIOUS: intentionally below accessible threshold; not a failure unless route was accessible.
- RUN FAIL: Any set where Check 12 averages <3 across finalists is not a complete run even if individual songs pass.
