# Simple Surface / Complex Engine — the ACCESSIBLE-arm doctrine

> **The surface must be graspable in ONE listen; the engine underneath must reward the TENTH.**
> The goal is not simplification. The goal is focused depth: recognizable surface, strange cathedral underneath.

This standard is **additive** (`skills/music/SKILL.md`): it raises song-force, mythic pressure, and panel routing without waiving one legacy package gate. It is judged by the 16-point gate in `skills/qa/references/suno_15_point_qa.md` — gates 1–7 test the Surface, gates 8–12 test the Engine.

## The two-layer contract

**The Surface (one listen buys it):**
- A **human singer** in a body, in a place, with an unsaid ache — not a system, not an aesthetic label.
- A **body-first opening**: the first four sung lines establish body / place / object / sensory pressure before any thesis.
- An **adoptable hook**: a stranger can sing the title phrase back after a 15–30 second clip; the chorus is a prayer, invocation, or confession — never policy or procedure.
- **Voice + pulse survival**: stripped to voice, pulse, and hook, the song still lands.

**The Engine (the tenth listen pays out):**
- **Golden Seed Alloy pressure** — the seed's lineage visibly changes hook, image, bridge, or production (see `golden_seed_alloy.md`).
- The **mythic image ladder** — ordinary → specific → strange → mythic → return to body — reaching the lyric or hook, not only a sidecar.
- **EMO dramaturgy** that transforms: the bridge and final chorus arrive somewhere the first verse could not have asserted.
- **Production dramaturgy** where every unusual sound has a job: cradle, cut, haunt, lift, rupture, contaminate, answer, resurrect, afterglow.

## Complexity routing — where the strangeness LIVES

The engine never crowds the doorway. Every complex move gets a **named routing slot**: hook (rarely), verse, bridge, production only, sidecar only, ghost bank. If a strange idea has no slot, it goes to the **ghost bank**, not the chorus. The attention ladder is the routing check: first 5 seconds, first hook memory, second-listen reward, deep-lore reward — each rung claimed by name.

## The two failure modes (fail both extremes)

- **Impressive obscurity** — an engine with no door. The panel admires it; nobody can sing it back. Blocking symptoms: no human singer, chorus-as-thesis, hook that cannot be hummed after one read.
- **Competent blandness** — a door with no house. Singable, forgettable, generic competent pop. Blocking symptoms: no mythic pressure reaching lyric/hook, production as the only reason the song works, no panel-forced change.

## Worked example

Seed strand: *a harbor pilot guiding the last container ship before the port automates.*
- **Surface:** she stands on the bridge wing at 4 a.m., salt on the railing, hook **"bring her in slow"** — graspable in one listen as a love-song-to-a-ship; the chorus is an instruction you can adopt for any careful goodbye.
- **Engine:** the one wounding fact — a harbor pilot reads the tide to the minute — sits at the bridge's hinge and the lyric answers it ("the water knew me back"). The image ladder runs railing → tide table → the port's cranes as sleeping herons → mythic (the harbor closes its one eye) → return to her cold hands. Production dramaturgy: the fog-horn drone *cradles* verse 1, is *cut* at the chorus, *haunts* the bridge pitched down a third, and *resurrects* under the last hook as the only accompaniment.
- **Routing:** the heron-crane image lives in the bridge (not the hook); the tide-table numerology is production-only (a 12.4-second delay throw); the automation polemic goes to the ghost bank — the song never argues, it docks.

One listen: a woman bringing a ship home. The tenth: a whole port, a whole ending, measured in tide-minutes.
